Niravaran Yatra

The Bare Trip

An Immersive Expedition into the Eastern Ghats

January 10-18, 2026 | ASR District, Andhra Pradesh

This is not a romanticized cultural tour. This is a raw, unfiltered immersion into the tribal belt of Andhra Pradesh's Eastern Ghats—experiencing the real, the good, the bad, and the ugly of tribal life during one of their most significant festivals. We'll live in tents, forage for food, cook with locals, and participate in Sankranti festivities that range from sacred celebrations to traditional cockfighting.

Two Goals

Constructive Access

Deepen relationships and create pathways for positive change that are community-led and culturally sensitive.

Community Building

Cultivate a group of 10 early adopters—risk-takers and co-creators ready to build decentralized trust and challenge conventional tourism models.

The 10-Day Journey

1

January 10 - Arrival

  • Reach Visakhapatnam by noon
  • Explore the coastal city
  • Prepare for the journey ahead
2-3

Jan 11-12 - Addathegala Sankranti Immersion

Cultural Experience:

  • Festival celebration and cooking dinner with locals
  • Traditional cockfighting
  • Fishtail palm toddy tasting

Nature Exploration:

  • Birding by Maddigedda Reservoir
  • River walk by Yeleru River
  • Foraging local greens and learning traditional cooking
4

Jan 13 - Paderu Medicinal Herb Workshop

  • Identify local herb ecology
  • Make traditional medicine by hand
  • Understand nature-rooted wellness
5-6

Jan 14-15 - Trek to Armakonda (Highest Peak)

  • Camp under open sky
  • Stargazing and sunrise above thick fog
  • Wild trails, natural springs, boulder formations
7

Jan 16 - Kandulaplem Waterfalls

  • Serene trek through pineapple farms
  • Lesser-known lush waterfalls
  • Swimming in natural pools
  • Authentic village food
8

Jan 17 - Araku Valley

Morning/Afternoon:

  • Scenic drive through Araku
  • Visit community seed bank with local seed conservator 'Bujji Amma'

Evening:

  • Caving at Borra Caves
  • Explore river gorges and waterfalls inside unexplored cave systems
  • Learn about geology and riverine ecology
  • Return to Visakhapatnam by late evening
9

January 18 - Departure

What Makes This Different

Raw Authenticity

No sanitized experiences. Live with communities as they are. Participate in actual traditions. Face the wild—hallucinogens, raw nature, unpredictable encounters.

Deep Immersion

Sleep in tents and village homestays. Forage and cook your own food. Learn traditional medicine-making. Experience festivals from the inside.

Physical Adventure

Trek to the highest peak in Eastern Ghats. Camp in wilderness. Navigate wild trails, springs, and boulders. River walks and waterfall exploration.

Accommodation & Logistics

Accommodation

  • Laya Guesthouse in Addathegala and Paderu (clean, hygienic, basic amenities)
  • Camping Setup during village stays and treks (all-weather tents, mats, sleeping bags)

Food

  • Locally cooked and sourced whenever possible
  • Trusted restaurants when necessary
  • Hands-on cooking with communities
  • Foraged greens and traditional preparations

Transport

  • Car/Bus with chauffeur for entire trip
  • Trip Leader: Trained Wilderness First Responder and Naturalist with deep local knowledge

Co-Creator Model

We're seeking 2-3 co-creators to help design and execute this journey.

Important: We're not looking for commercial partners. We want people driven by genuine interest and curiosity—those who want to contribute to the entire trip because it resonates with them.

What Co-Creation Means:

Complete Transparency: Full access to all planning, financial details, and decision-making
Shared Responsibility: You're among the organizers on the ground with the group
Active Participation: Contribute your knowledge, network, or planning expertise throughout
No Financial Exchange: We're not charging you, and we're not paying you
Profit Sharing (if any): If there are profits from the trip, we'll happily share them

What We're Looking For:

Genuine interest over commercial motivation
Risk-takers and executors
Deep knowledge (ecology, tribal culture, wilderness skills, etc.)
Network or know-how for trip planning
Commitment to authenticity over polish
Willingness to build decentralized trust

The Philosophy

This itinerary balances rural cultural immersion and practices, outdoor exploration and adventure, and agriculture and traditional knowledge.

With a rhythm of physical activity and adventure followed by calm and restful exploration and deep learning and connection.

Note: The itinerary is tentative and can be optimized through discussion.
